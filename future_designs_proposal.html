Harmony Health System

Future Clinical Workspace Designs

A complete architectural proposal and stakeholder presentation for Phase 2 and Phase 3 expansion of the Harmony Patient Record Workspace.

Phase 1: Baseline Visual UX (Implemented)

Current Active Upgrades

The patient workspace tabs have been upgraded with beautiful, micro-animated visual modules that are live in production. These additions require no server changes and run with high-performance client rendering:

Overview: Critical Allergy Alert

A glassmorphic, glowing warning card at the top of the Overview panel, rendering active clinical allergies and contraindications safely.

  • Type-safe React boundary integrations
  • Double-pulse warning highlight framework
  • Instant visibility on clinical file loading

Vitals: Custom SVG Trend Charts

Dual responsive chart tracking Systolic/Diastolic BP (rose-sky gradients) and Weight (emerald line) over the last 10 visits.

  • Zero external NPM package overhead
  • Fully responsive with high-DPI hover guides
  • Immune to Next.js SSR hydration drift

Remedies: Patient Guidelines Printing

One-click Treatment Leaflets printing. Automatically formats remedies, dietary instructions, and guidelines beautifully on a physical page.

  • Custom CSS @media print layout profiles
  • Clean high-contrast typography
  • Isolates the leaflet text, hiding administrative UI

Notes: Clinical Handovers Bento Grid

Structured clinical handovers arranged into themed sticky-note grids: Administrative, Pharmacological, Genetic, and Chronological notes.

  • Highly scannable CSS Grid bento layout
  • Role-stamped colors (cream, sky, emerald, lavender)
  • Checked-state click actions for archiving
Phase 2: Structured Clinical Tooling (Proposed Future)

Clinical Decision Support Modules

Phase 2 shifts from static text inputs to active decision-support elements, enabling standardized diagnostic tracking and system-by-system physical reviews.

🩺 Assessments Tab: System-by-System Exam Templates

Transitions raw physical examination fields into physiological system accordions (HEENT, Cardiovascular, Respiratory, GI, Neuro, Skin).

  • Smart-Insert Chips: One-click clinical templates that automatically populate standard observations (e.g. clicking "Normal GI" inserts: "Abdomen soft, non-tender, non-distended, active bowel sounds.").
  • Comparative Slider Panels: Lock historical exam reports side-by-side to review physical improvement.

🛡️ Diagnosis Tab: ICD-11 Workbench & DDx Workspace

Integrates diagnostic coding and active case synthesis directly into the practitioner's workspace.

  • ICD-11 Directory Search: Auto-completing query search to tag WHO clinical codes (e.g. "Essential Hypertension" - BA41) onto diagnoses.
  • Differential Diagnosis (DDx) Whiteboard: Drafting canvas to list suspect, working, and ruled-out conditions before promotion to primary.
Phase 3: Intake Automation & Sensory Mapping (Proposed Future)

Structured Cabinets & Anatomical Body Mapping

Phase 3 completes the workspace with secure intake document storage, qualitative scoring trends, and highly advanced spatial injury tracking.

📅 Follow-ups: Subjective Scores & SMS Recalls

Charts qualitative patient progress alongside objective vital data, with recall outreach automation.

  • Subjective Score Trends: Tracks patient-rated metrics (Sleep, Energy, Mood, Appetite) on 1-10 timelines.
  • Auto-Recall SMS: Compiles the next scheduled date, doctor, and symptom into pre-formatted clinic SMS/WhatsApp templates.

📁 Documents: Organized Lab Cabinets & Signature Pads

A full digital filing cabinet organizing files into structured drawers.

  • Category Sub-drawers: Direct organization into Consent, Lab Reports, Radiology, and Medical Certificates.
  • Witness signature module: On-screen digital verification captures signatures for legal, high-risk, or parental agreements.

🧍 Interactive Visual Body Mapping (High-Fidelity Concept)

This proposed module replaces textual symptom location notes with an interactive SVG anatomical coordinate map in the Assessments workspace. Doctors double-click body regions to pinpoint pain, skin lesions, or tension, mapping those exact points on chronological progress graphs.
